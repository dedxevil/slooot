Gratuity Calculator Guide

💡 What is Gratuity?

Gratuity is a sum of money paid by an employer to an employee as a reward for long-standing service. As per the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, any organization with 10 or more employees must pay gratuity to eligible employees.

Gratuity is also applicable in cases of:

  • Retirement
  • Superannuation
  • Resignation after 5+ years
  • Sudden death or disability

🧑‍💼 Who is eligible for gratuity?

You're eligible for gratuity if:

  • You have completed 5 or more years in the same organization
  • You retire or resign after this period
  • You face disability due to work
  • You are eligible for superannuation

💰 Is there a limit on the gratuity amount?

Yes. The Government of India has imposed a maximum limit of ₹20,00,000 (₹20 lakhs) for tax-free gratuity.

⏳ How long will it take to receive my gratuity?

Employers must disburse gratuity within 30 days from the full & final settlement.
If delayed, the employer must pay interest:

  • 8% interest if paid within 4 weeks
  • 10% interest if delay exceeds 4 weeks

📊 What is the formula for gratuity?

The formula differs based on whether your company is covered under the Gratuity Act:

  • Covered under the Act: Gratuity = (Basic + DA) × 15 × Completed Years / 26
  • Not covered under the Act: Gratuity = (Basic + DA) × 15 × Completed Years / 30
